Last updated December 12, 2022

In the software Industry, the skills that a developer is assessed on during interviews are different than what he/she works on in their day job. I recently had an interview with a well-known company that started with a coding challenge consisting of two simple challenges and given a 30 minute time frame. This was my first introduction to the Hacker Rank platform that seems to be so popular nowadays for assessing the coding skills of developers. .

Coding Test VS. Day-To-Day as a Developer

We are typically evaluated on our aptitude for problem-solving, data structures, and algorithms throughout the interviews. In our day jobs, we don’t have to invert binary trees or calculate the shortest path between two nodes in a graph

According to the Recruiter, The Position was supposedly required Heavy (at least three years) Javascript, and I have about 7 total combined years since being introduced to the coding language.

Older Coding Experience | Day-To_Day

During my last remote role as a JR Web Developer, I was mainly working on maintenance tasks and fixing code/Debugging and wasn’t really doing a whole lot of "New" coding. The tech stack was old and no big projects were given to our team.

Some responsibilities

  • Mentoring junior developers
  • Checking Quality of your team’s codebase
  • Getting involved in org-level discussions
  • Helping solve production issues on priority, etc

💡 Inspiration

Interview Tips

  • Prepare.
  • Practice thinking out loud.
  • Practice writing code by hand.
  • Understand the problem before coding.
  • Stalker (1979)
  • Nostalghia (1983)
  • The Sacrifice (1986)

Althrough I did prepare before hand with some coding challenges and typical front-end interview questions to be expected, I found the assessment very time constraining providing only 30 minutes for 2 Coding Questions or Challenges related to Front-End Development and javascript.
I have 7+ years of Javascript experience as well as 10 years of coding experience in general yet still felt pressured while trying to work out these solutions with the limited time clock. I feel that If I were'nt constantly focused on the Time aspect, I would have been able to more effectively focus on implementing my solution to the questions/challenges.
The main reason for this blog post is to finish those coding challenges here and demonstrate my ability to solve problems effectively.

The first Challenge: "The Most Poetic Word"

Create a function which takes a poem and identifies the most poetic word in the poem and returns this back to the user as output. There are three criteria, Number of Vowels, Presence of K or J, order precedent( Words that come earlier score higher) subtract -1 for every k and -2 for every j
We consider a word, to be beautiful if the following two conditions are satisfied: No two consecutive characters are the same. No two consecutive characters are in the following vowel set: a, e, i, o, u, y. Note that we consider y to be a vowel in this challenge.

The second challenge: Flexblox

Position flowers a certain way using flexbox CSS Property
